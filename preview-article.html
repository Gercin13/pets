The Complete Guide to Pet Vaccination

Dr. Anna Smirnova Verified expert
8 min read·July 2, 2026

Vaccination is the single most effective thing you can do to protect your pet from preventable, often fatal disease. But between core and non-core shots, boosters, and titer tests, the schedule can feel overwhelming. Here's the plain-language version.

Why the timing matters

A puppy or kitten's early immunity comes from its mother's milk — protection that fades over the first few months. Vaccines bridge that gap, and giving them too early or too late leaves a dangerous window open. That's why the first year follows a tighter schedule than adulthood.

Core vs. non-core vaccines

Core vaccines protect against diseases that are widespread, severe, or transmissible to humans — every pet should receive them. Non-core vaccines are recommended based on lifestyle and region: a dog that hikes and boards needs more coverage than one that rarely leaves the apartment.

A vaccinated pet isn't just protected — it protects every animal it meets. Herd immunity starts at home.

— Dr. Anna Smirnova

What to expect afterward

Mild tiredness or a little tenderness at the injection site is normal for a day. Contact your vet promptly if you notice:

  • Swelling of the face or muzzle
  • Repeated vomiting or diarrhea
  • Difficulty breathing
