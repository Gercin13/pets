Done right, a crate becomes your dog's own safe den — never a place to be sent as punishment.

## Go slower than feels necessary

Start with the door open, feeding meals inside so the crate means **good things happen here**. Rushing this step is the single biggest reason crate training fails.

## A routine that builds trust

- Toss treats inside throughout the day, no pressure to enter
- Feed every meal in the crate once your dog goes in willingly
- Close the door for a few seconds at a time, then release calmly
- Only extend crate time once your dog settles without whining

> A crate your dog chooses to nap in on its own is proof the training worked.
