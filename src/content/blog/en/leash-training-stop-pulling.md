A dog that pulls isn't being stubborn — it's just walking at its natural pace while learning yours doesn't come naturally yet.

## The "be a tree" method

The instant the leash goes tight, stop walking completely and wait. Only move again once there's slack — dogs quickly learn that pulling makes the walk *slower*, not faster.

## Habits that make it stick

- Practice in a low-distraction space before tackling a busy street
- Reward loose-leash walking generously, not just perfect heel position
- Keep sessions short and end on a success, not on frustration
- Use the same cue and leash length every time for consistency

> Calm, boring consistency beats a clever trick every time — dogs learn from patterns, not lectures.
