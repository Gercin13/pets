Bag fronts are marketing; the real answers are in the small print on the back.

## Ingredients are listed by weight

The first three to five ingredients make up most of the food, so look for a **named animal protein** (not a vague "meat by-product") near the top of the list.

## Beyond the ingredient list

- Check the **guaranteed analysis** for protein, fat, and fibre percentages
- Look for an **AAFCO** (or local equivalent) statement confirming it's complete and balanced
- Treat words like "natural" or "premium" as marketing, not regulated claims
- Match the life-stage label — puppy, adult, senior — to your pet's actual age

> If you can't picture the first ingredient as real food, that's worth a second look.
