Neither format is universally "better" — the right choice depends on your pet's health, habits, and your budget.

## What each format does well

**Wet food** adds meaningful water intake, which matters for pets prone to urinary issues. **Dry food** is more calorie-dense, keeps longer once opened, and can help slow tartar buildup through the chewing action.

## A practical way to decide

- Cats prone to urinary crystals often do better with more wet food
- Free-feeding a busy household usually works better with kibble
- Mixing both can offset the downsides of either on its own
- Always check the label for a complete-and-balanced statement, regardless of format

> The best diet is the one your pet eats consistently, tolerates well, and that fits how you actually feed them.
